For the first week I'm focusing on 1. what is 'drying' and 2. How do I want to represent it? 3. What tool/software would best show this?

I did some diving into examples of drying and its relation to climate change. I thought I would do something with changing water levels... But what caught my eye was the physics of what 'drying' actually is.

I know next to nothing about physics/chemistry... But my basic understanding is that as a heat source gets closer to liquid, the 'heat' causes the particles to move faster and faster, eventually separating them and causing them to spread up into the air.

Above is a diagram (from a literal children's website) explaining the concept.

I'm starting out in p5.js to do a simplified version of the physics of evaporation.

My first attempt has an orange circle representing 'heat', and a couple white particles, representing 'liquid'.

As the heat source gets closer to the particles (controlled by my mouse) the particles begin to shake and move more and more.

I wanted to really simulate not just the particles moving, but the actual evaporation. I added floatSpeed to make the particles not only shake, but float away up into the air when the heat source gets closer.
